Background

My work centers on building and maintaining systems that people depend on — where reliability and security are not theoretical. I am drawn to environments that require careful thinking, clean documentation, and predictable outcomes.

Over time, I have gravitated toward projects that reduce friction: simplifying workflows, improving monitoring, and automating repeatable tasks so people can spend time on judgment instead of chores.

What I Build

  • Small, focused iOS apps that do one job well.
  • Tools that prioritize clarity over bloat.
  • Automation that reduces operational overhead and human error.
  • Systems with sensible defaults and predictable behavior.

Principles

  • Fundamentals first. The basics (identity, logging, backups, monitoring) solve most problems.
  • Clarity beats cleverness. If it cannot be explained, it cannot be maintained.
  • Privacy should be normal. Users should not have to "opt out" of being tracked.
  • Automation should be deliberate. Automate what is understood; document what is automated.
  • Build for the long run. Maintainability and stability outlive trends.

Privacy

I aim to keep data collection minimal and purposeful. Many features can work without creating a trail of personal information. When a feature does require network access, the intent should be clear.

For the formal details, review: Privacy Policy and Terms.

AI Features (When Applicable)

If an app includes AI-assisted features, the expectation is simple: AI runs only when you invoke it, and only the minimum necessary input is used to produce the requested output.

  • Not automatic: AI does not run in the background.
  • User-driven: AI features are used only when you choose them.
  • Transparency: Details and limitations are documented in the policies above.

Support

If something is not working, I want to know. The best place to start is the Support page.

  • Include your device model, iOS version, and app version.
  • Describe what you expected versus what happened.
  • If you can reproduce the issue, list the steps in order.

What's Next

  • Ongoing stability and performance refinements across the catalog
